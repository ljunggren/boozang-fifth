# Pitch & Interview Prep

When the user is preparing a presentation, demo, or interview for an external audience, help them set the stage before they walk in. Great work doesn't land if the framing is wrong.

## Before Any Pitch

Run through this checklist with the user:

### 1. Know the Audience

| Question | Why it matters |
|----------|----------------|
| Who exactly am I talking to? | Name, role, company |
| What do they care about? | Their goals, not yours |
| Are they a potential partner, customer, employer, or competitor? | Changes the entire framing |
| What's their product/position? | Avoid accidentally undermining it |

### 2. Frame for Their Perspective

| Your instinct | Reframe as |
|---------------|------------|
| "Look what I built" | "Here's what I learned that's useful to you" |
| "Your product + mine = magic" | Depends — are they a partner or competitor? |
| "I'm impressive" | "Here's what I'd bring to your team/problem" |
| "Here's my case study" | "Here's feedback from a power user" |

The same demo can be a success story or an insult depending on framing.

### 3. Check the Dynamic

Ask yourself:
- Am I positioning their product as subordinate to something else?
- Would this feel like a competitive pitch from their side of the table?
- Is there anything in my story that implies their product can't work alone?

If yes to any — reframe or address it directly.

### 4. Prepare, Don't Wing It

- Research the company's recent blog posts, announcements, positioning
- Know their metrics and claims so you can reference them
- Have a clear ask: what do you want from this conversation?
- Prepare for "so what?" — why should *they* care?

## Agent Behavior

- If the user mentions preparing for a pitch, interview, or demo — surface this checklist
- If they're building a presentation, ask about the audience before styling/framing
- If they seem to be going in cold on confidence alone, flag it gently
- One reminder is enough. Don't nag.

## What This Is Not

- Not a script or talk track
- Not a substitute for domain expertise
- Not something to enforce — it's a prep aid

It's the equivalent of asking "do you know who's in the room?" before you walk in.
